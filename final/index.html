The Smashing Pumpkins

The Smashing Pumpkins are an American alternative rock band formed in Chicago in 1988, led by singer, songwriter, and guitarist Billy Corgan. Known for their expansive sound that blends elements of dream pop, gothic rock, heavy metal, and psychedelia, the band rose to fame in the early 1990s as a defining force in alternative music.

Their breakthrough came with the 1993 album Siamese Dream, praised for its lush guitar layers and emotional depth, followed by the ambitious double album Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness (1995), which cemented their place in rock history.
