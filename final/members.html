Members

Billy Corgan

Role
Lead vocals, guitar, songwriting
Bio
Billy Corgan is the creative force behind The Smashing Pumpkins, known for his distinctive voice, introspective lyrics, and visionary leadership of the band. As the principal songwriter, Corgan shaped the band's sound from their formation in 1988, steering them through various stylistic phases. His often intense and introspective lyrics explore themes of alienation, love, and identity. Outside of the band, Corgan has pursued solo music, business ventures, and even a stint as the owner of the National Wrestling Alliance.

James Iha

Role
Guitar, backing vocals
Bio
James Iha is the guitarist and one of the founding members of The Smashing Pumpkins. Known for his melodic playing style, Iha contributed to many of the band’s most memorable tracks, offering a contrast to Corgan’s heavy guitar tones with his smoother, more atmospheric sound. He also provided backing vocals and wrote several songs for the band. After leaving the band in the early 2000s, Iha pursued a solo career and collaborated with various artists. He rejoined The Smashing Pumpkins in 2018.

D'arcy Wretzky

Role
Bass guitar, backing vocals
Bio
D'arcy Wretzky was the bassist and one of the founding members of The Smashing Pumpkins. She contributed to the band's early sound with her steady basslines and backing vocals. Wretzky played a key role in shaping the band's identity, particularly in the Siamese Dream and Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness eras. She left the band in 1999 and has since kept a low profile, though her contributions remain integral to the band's sound during its most influential period.

Jimmy Chamberlin

Role
Drums
Bio
Jimmy Chamberlin is the drummer and one of the longest-serving members of The Smashing Pumpkins. Known for his technical skill and dynamic drumming style, Chamberlin played a crucial role in the band's signature sound, particularly during their peak years. His work on albums like Siamese Dream and Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness is widely praised for its intensity and complexity. Chamberlin was a central figure in the band's early success and later rejoined them after a hiatus, remaining an essential part of their lineup.
